Project Description

Lipids are used in many different product formulations. Industries such as the cosmeceutical and pharmaceutical industries require supplies of certain lipid classes for the formulation of their final products.

Our group developed a process that separated 3 different lipid classes, Phospholipids, Glycolipids, and Neutral Lipids, from Nannochloropsis oculata, a marine-water single-cell microalga. The process involved a microwave-based pre-treatment step, a liquid-liquid extraction unit with ethanol and supercritical CO2 solvents, and additional separation stages using flash columns and a silica packed column.

  • Goals
    • Develop a process to extract lipids from microalgae
    • Model the process with a flowsheet simulator
    • Perform an economic analysis to determine profitability
